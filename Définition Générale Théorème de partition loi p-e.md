The **Partition Theorem (also known as the P-E Monfette Law)** is a foundational theoretical principle in additive number theory, specifically applied to the modular structure of prime numbers.

#### General Definition

The **Partition Theorem (P-E Law)** states that any even integer $N$ greater than a critical threshold does not decompose randomly into the sum of two prime numbers (the Goldbach conjecture framework) but instead follows a strict geometric and statistical distribution governed by cyclic invariants called **modular highways**.

In this model, the partition operates within the structural framework of **Modulo 30** (the product of the first three prime numbers: $2 \times 3 \times 5$).

   $$\text{R30 Highways} = \{1, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23, 29\}$$

   $$p \equiv a \pmod{30} \quad \text{and} \quad q \equiv b \pmod{30} \quad \text{where } a+b \equiv N \pmod{30}$$
